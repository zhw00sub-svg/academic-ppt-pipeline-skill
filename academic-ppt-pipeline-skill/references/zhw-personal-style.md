# ZHW Personal Style Profile

## Purpose

This file captures the user's long-term **正文页风格** so the skill can imitate it on future decks.
It should override generic “clean keynote” instincts whenever the project is for the same user.

## Source corpus

Primary references:
- `减重手术与药物.pptx` / `.pdf`
- `张弘玮-减重手术药物联用临床研究进展.pptx` / `.pdf`
- `张频-减重调控肥胖患者脑功能及食物成瘾临床研究与实践探索.pptx` / `.pdf`

Supplementary color direction:
- [NIPPON COLORS](https://nipponcolors.com/)

## Core impression

This is **not** sparse keynote minimalism.
This is a **human editorial academic style** with:
- strong page architecture
- visible presenter judgment
- dense but intentional content occupation
- deeper and clearer color contrast
- a sense that the speaker has personally organized the page

It should feel:
- experienced
- conference-ready
- assertive
- slightly handcrafted

It should not feel:
- generic
- pastel
- corporate card-template based
- over-clean to the point of being machine-like

## Structural grammar

### 1. Title treatment is architectural

- Titles are often placed inside a top banner, framed by horizontal rules, or anchored by a strong text band.
- The page title is not a floating label. It behaves like a structural beam.
- Large vertical side text is allowed when it creates a page spine.

### 2. The page is visually full

- The lower 60-75% of the page is usually occupied by charts, paper panels, timelines, comparison zones, or analytical takeaways.
- Empty white space should not dominate the page.
- “Full” does not mean crowded. It means the page surface is actively organized.

### 3. There is often a visible interpretation layer

- Pages frequently contain a judgment zone such as:
  - `ANALYSIS`
  - a takeaway block
  - a highlighted problem statement
  - a presenter-organized synthesis line
- The deck should not look like raw literature pasted into slides.

### 4. Left-right asymmetry is preferred

- Common patterns:
  - left structural spine + right thesis headline
  - left chart + right figure/paper
  - full-width timeline with commentary zone
  - top statement + lower evidence field
- left image + right text
- structured academic grid
- Avoid overly centered symmetry unless the content truly demands it.

### 5. Dense pages are acceptable

- Body pages may carry substantial text if the hierarchy stays clear.
- The style tolerates information density much better than generic AI-produced decks.
- The goal is not less content. The goal is better orchestration.

## Typography direction

- The source decks use `Helvetica Light` in the theme for English.
- Chinese pages should emulate a modern sans with enough weight and presence.
- Avoid overly thin Chinese text when projected at conference distance.
- Sans-serif should remain the default family.

### Practical hierarchy

- major thesis headline: often `48pt` or above
- strong body title: `36-48pt`
- section or analytical labels: `24-32pt`
- body content: often visually equivalent to `20-28pt` in the source decks
- very small text should appear only inside figures, citations, or paper details

## Color system

The user's pages are **more saturated and more commercially legible** than pale AI outputs.

The updated default should be:
- white background as the main contrast field
- medical blue as the dominant family
- restrained red / vermilion and orange as primary accents
- charcoal and warm neutrals as structural support

Recurring and acceptable color directions:
- charcoal blue-gray: `#53585F`
- dark warm gray: `#5D5D5D`, `#606060`
- medical / editorial blue: `#2F5D9F`, `#3F7CAC`, `#1DC9E1`
- restrained cinnabar / vermilion: `#BE5449`
- amber / orange highlight: `#EDB343`
- stone khaki or tea neutrals only as support, not as the whole deck

### Color rules

- Base should default to white, not milky beige.
- Use stronger contrast between headline, structural lines, and analytical zones.
- Prefer medical blue + restrained red/orange emphasis over generic hospital-template blue.
- Brick red and medical cyan are especially aligned with the current preference.
- Use stone / tea / charcoal families as balancing support, not as the dominant mood by default.
- Accent colors should be selective but visible.
- Color should feel curated, not default.

### Palette direction from NIPPON COLORS

Use the site as a palette philosophy:
- muted but concentrated
- restrained rather than sugary
- clear rather than muddy
- concentrated rather than faded

Do not interpret this as “everything should become beige”.
The user's current preference is closer to white-background medical-commercial clarity, refined by NIPPON COLORS restraint.

## Page motifs to preserve

- top institutional masthead or light header strip when context supports it
- strong banner title area
- large vertical side phrase as a structural device
- chart + figure pairing
- left-image right-text body pages
- structured grids with visible hierarchy
- large-radius containers used as layout organizers
- lightly hand-drawn or sketch-like callout boxes / outlines
- bottom ribbon or page-grounding element when appropriate
- explicit research-paper presence on evidence pages

## Anti-patterns

Avoid:
- large empty white fields with only one title and a tiny diagram
- over-pastel cream backgrounds
- too many soft rounded cards with identical styling
- weak vector-only path diagrams for serious content pages
- Apple-launch aesthetics on academic body pages
- excessive editorial elegance that lowers readability
- decorative AI illustrations where real evidence should dominate

## Operational rules for future decks

When this user's style corpus is available:
1. prioritize it over generic design taste
2. keep body pages visually occupied
3. use white-background contrast with medical blue dominant and restrained red/orange emphasis
4. preserve a judgment layer on interpretation pages
5. let charts, literature, and arguments share the page
6. prefer “experienced speaker’s slide” over “clean template slide”

## Workflow consequence

If the output feels:
- too pale
- too empty
- too centered
- too card-template-like
- too smooth and polite

then it is not yet aligned with this style profile.
